Exploring my Favorite Stories

The Way of Kings The Boys About Me Home

Summary

Eric Kripke and Garth Ennis' The Boys is a superhero TV show that explores what happens when superheroes, or "supes", are corrupted by fame and corperate interests. It follows Hughie, whose girlfriend was accidentally murdered by a superhero. He meets some other people who know of the supe-corruption and works with them to topple the corperate superhero empire. He ends up falling in love with Annie ("Starlight"), who is a supe new to the industry and is therefore untainted by the corruption. Together, they all work in the shadows to kill the corrupt supes meanwhile trying to avoid Homelander, the invincible face of Vought (The company behind the supes).

Purpose of the Story

I believe the purpose of this story is to serve as a critique of corperate greed and celebrity culture. The Boys explores morality and asks the question of what makes someone good or evil. Some important topics are humanity's darker impulses, resistence against oppression, and the thin line between justice and vengeance. I believe the writers wanted us to realize that innocent people who stand against corperations stand to lose their livelihoods today, even if it happens in alleyways and courtrooms rather than being done by superheroes.

Influences/Connections

Though we didn't watch any superhero movies in this course, I think a connection can easily be made to The Queen of Versailles or to Breaking Bad. In the former, the Siegel family (Primarily Jackie Siegel) has a similar demeanor to the Supes, one of entitlement. The Boys is also comparable to Breaking Bad in the antics of the main characters.Both Walter and Hughie's gang to behind the back of the law in order to either provide for or get revenge for their families, doing unspeakable things in the name of justice or happiness.

Cultural/Societal Relevance

Corperate Greed/Abuse of Power

Vought greatly resembles many of the big companies today that make use of their lawyers and money to control people. Nintendo and Disney are two companies known for being incredibly merciless towards anyone whom they view as even slightly infringing upon their IP. These lawyers can be compared to how Vought uses the supes to kill anyone who stands against Vought's plans.

Propaganda

The Boys shows how public opinion is incredibly easy to sway. Most people in the show are cluelessly unaware of what is going on behind the scenes, and rally behind Vought and the supes. Vought has their paws on every industry of media and uses their reach to brainwash the people into buying their lies.

Narrative Strategies

Competing/Dual Narratives are used by contrasting the perspectives of Hughie and his team, and that of the corperate-controlled supes. Both sides believe they are doing the right thing and are the face of justice, yet both resort to murder and violence to accomplish their goals.

Why I picked this story

I chose The Boys because of how refreshing it is. It takes the superhero genre and entirely flips it on its head so that it can tackle some modern societal issues. It does this while mixing in action, violence, and humor. The show doesn't shy away from comfronting uncomfortable truths and turns a bland and overused genre into something truly unique.
